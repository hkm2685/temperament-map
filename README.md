This code builds some interactivity into an existing SVG map of temperaments I have created; play with it at https://hkm2685.github.io/temperament-map/rank2/viewer.

The left section of the map functions as a graph where the y-axis represents [generator](<https://en.xen.wiki/w/Generator>) size in [cents](https://en.xen.wiki/w/Cent) and the x-axis represents number of times the generator must be stacked to reach a prime. The triangles corresponding to all primes span 15 cents (12 cents for the half-octave map, and 10 for the third-octave) for visibility and so that the viewer can get a sense of the error of each temperament; the OKLCH hue of the text and triangles correspond linearly to the prime harmonic's position within the octave.

The right section of the map displays every rank-2 [temperament](https://en.xen.wiki/w/Mathematical_theory_of_regular_temperaments) on Xen Wiki as of June 9, 2026 on a page with Category:Rank 2. Temperaments with different [subgroups](https://en.xen.wiki/w/Just_intonation_subgroup) but the same name are listed as one. The temperaments are listed according to the [CWE](https://en.xen.wiki/w/Constrained_tuning) tuning of their largest subgroup, and the lowest [Sintel's badness](https://en.xen.wiki/w/Tenney%E2%80%93Euclidean_temperament_measures#TE_logflat_badness) of all subgroups is listed in parentheses.
